Richard & Commando

Mashatu Tent Camp · Mashatu · Botswana

Guiding since 1994Big CatsElephant BehaviourTrack & SignLeopard

Richard was born in 1966 in the Northern Tuli Block — not near this landscape, but inside it. Before Mashatu Tent Camp had a name that appeared in travel magazines, this land was simply where he grew up.

He started guiding in 1994. That is thirty years in the same piece of Botswana — tracking the same predator corridors, learning which individual animals use which routes.

At 59, Richard reads the bush the way a musician reads a score — fluidly, ahead of the beat, already knowing what is coming before it arrives.

Photographer's Note · John JansonRichard saw the leopard before I did. He had already framed the shot before I had raised the camera. Thirty years in this specific piece of Africa has produced something that no training course produces.

Commando

Then there is Commando. The tracker. At 45, Commando has been tracking at Mashatu since 2008 — seventeen years of following animals through one of southern Africa's most demanding terrains.

Watching Commando work is watching someone in complete mastery of a discipline that is almost impossible to teach. He reconstructs the last few hours of an animal's life from the evidence it leaves without meaning to.

A father of three, Commando is quiet in the way people are quiet when they are thinking about something more important than conversation.

Photographer's Note · John JansonThe leopard on the riverbank exists because of Commando. He found the tracks. He read the direction. He understood where she had come from and where she was going.
