Safari Guide · Serolo Camp · Tuli Block · Botswana

Jou

Serolo Camp · Tuli Wilderness · Botswana

Brown Hyena TrackingLarge PredatorsNocturnal WildlifeTrack & SignBird IdentificationTuli Block

Jou is one of those guides you do not forget. Not because of the animals he finds — though he finds them — but because of how he changes the way you look at the land around you. By the end of a drive with Jou, you are not scanning the bush for movement. You are reading it.

At 63, he continues to guide through the Tuli Block with the same physical commitment and focus you would expect from someone half his age. This is not a career winding down. It is a career at full depth — every year of experience compounding into something that simply cannot be taught in a classroom or replicated from a field guide.

His relationship with the Tuli Block goes beyond professional knowledge. This landscape — the ancient riverbeds, the basalt outcrops, the mopane woodland, the dry pans — is territory he has spent a lifetime learning. He knows where the brown hyenas move after dark. He knows which pan the lion uses at first light. He knows when the bee-eaters will come back to the riverbank and which direction to look when the Verreaux's eagle crosses the sky.

Behind the guiding is a father — a man who has spent his career not just tracking wildlife but providing, quietly and without complaint, for the people he loves.

Photographer's Note · John JansonA good safari guide changes the way you see. With Jou, every drive in the Tuli Block became a lesson in patience, attention and reading the land. He did not just find the hyena — he taught me how to wait for her. The images from those nights exist because of his skill, his knowledge of this specific piece of Africa, and his ability to be completely still when stillness was required.
